LIFE AND WANDER

WHO AM I

I like to define myself as a Civil Engineer 'with feelings'. I am walking the journey of life like a shooting star in slow motion. And to answer your question, my name is Fran Sanchez, the Spirit behind The Beach Lab.

THIRST FOR LEARNING

Measured by traditional education standards, I could probably be tagged one of the worst -if not the worst- student in my University. Which is kind of interesting, because I love learning. I used to skip class to run to the library. Libraries are my kind of place. I devour books, any kind of book. The more I read, the more I want to know. It's a sense of urgency. So much to learn, so little time. I never start a book from the first page, I open a random one. Sometimes I read backwards, sometimes I scan words jumping across chapters, sometimes I jump into another book. That's is how I ended up doing so different and apparently unrelated things, From Ocean Yatchmaster and diving, to engineering and math, to music and arts, to entepreneurship and many more to come. And yet, I could probably be one of the worst student in my University.

NEED FOR DISCOVERY

The real voyage of discovery consists not in seeking new landscapes, but in having new eyes.
Marcel Proust

The day I sequence my DNA I will check for a gene known as 7R. In 2013, National Geographic highlighted that this particular gene is a critical marker for an explorer. Feeling joy when traveling to remote locations is just a consequence of it. Exploration is about feeling comfortable with the unknown, the hidden. It's about experiencing pleasure in the quest rather than the discovery itself. It's about becoming a pioneer by unwinding the hints you find on the way. It applies to every aspect in life. You are an explorer when you develop your own tools and computer software rather than using existing solutions. You are an explorer when you prefer looking through the eyepiece of a telescope or a microscope rather than accessing a catalog of images. You are an explorer when you seek for forgotten wisdom in ancient civilizations. You are an explorer when you try to solve the misteries of mystic and out of logic phenomena. Exploration is the essence of humanity.

LOVE FOR NATURE

There's something about this planet that produces a strong feeling on me. It goes beyond any logic fascination. It is some sort of energy encoded in every organic and inorganic matter. Even now I can feel it. It's in every drop of the seas, in every dust particle of a mountain, in the air we breathe. It's in what we can see and it's in what we can't. It's everywhere, and I love it. I love this planet and I will stand for it. I am not giving up on Earth, I am not looking forward to go to Mars. I will use all my intelligence, all my skills, all my forces, all the energy I borrowed, to preserve, improve and leave this planet a better place than I found it. I will transmit my sons the passion I have for nature so the cycle will continue. That is my manifesto.

PASSION FOR CULTURES

Blend like an Octopus. Flow like the Wind. Absorb like a Sponge. I am lucky enough to have a job that allows me to experience very diverse and rich cultures across the World. For me it's so important to understand and embrace other people's cultures. It's not only about the treasure of wisdom, but also encounter new forms of intelligence. Intelligence adopts many formats, sometimes very subtle. On the dark side, neglecting other cultural beings lead to missunderstandings, fear and hate. The perfect ingredients for people's manipulation and one of our modern societies sins. Cultural awareness balances three fundamental concepts. Identity has an inbound attractor that seeks the uniqueness of the group or invididual. Diversity, on the other side, is an outbound force that repels from any other. A third element, Complementarity, gives balance and harmony to the whole. The beauty of multiculturalism is that you don't need to abandon your own culture to love, appreaciate and respect other people's cultures.
