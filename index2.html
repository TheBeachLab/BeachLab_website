About The Beach Lab

Welcome to The Beach Lab, a Fab(ulous) Lab(oratory) for research and development in digital fabrication and soon also in synthetic biology participant in a global network of interconnected Fab Labs.

Fab Labs began in 2001 as an outreach project from MIT’s Center for Bits and Atoms and since then they have spread over the world. The Center for Bits and Atoms assembled millions of dollars in machines for research in digital fabrication, ultimately aiming at developing programmable molecular assemblers that will be able to make almost anything. Today’s fab labs fall between these extremes, comprising roughly one hundred thousand dollars in equipment and materials that can be used today to do what will be possible with tomorrow’s personal fabricators.

Activities in our lab range from educational programs to consumer products development to local business and administrations problem-solving to coastal and marine research. Projects that can be developed and produced in The Beach Lab include environmental technology like solar and wind-powered turbines, underwater robots for ocean sensing and exploration and wireless sensor networks.

The Beach Lab is one of the roughly 50 Laboratories in the world participant in MIT’s Center for Bits and Atoms educational programs, providing access to Fab Academy HTMAA (How to make almost anything) and Bio Academy HTGAA (How to grow almost anything) courses.

The Future is Green

It's not about the rewards. It's not about the punishments either. This is all abouth the consequences. The Beach Lab is a US Green Buiding Council member and the first digital fabrication space in the world registered under the LEED Green Building rating program to pursue LEED certification. Digital fabrication is used to monitor and control energy, water comsumption and waste providing real time and long term open data. So you wanted anyway.

Flip Flap: Open Source Mechanical Split Flap Display

Project Description:

Flip Flap is an open source mechanical Split Flap display like those in train station and airports.

This project was released in Maker Faire Rome 2014 and is going to be soon pushed to a next level through kickstarter.

Project Details:

  • Client: Beach Lab
  • Documentation and files: Beach Lab's Github
  • Date: October 2014

Light Cycle: Open Source Persistence of Vision Light Cycle

Project Description:

This project came after a 3 years old kid asked us to help him to become Tron.

The first milestone is to build a light cycle addon for his bike so that he can enter The Grid.
