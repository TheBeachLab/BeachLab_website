SELF-ADAPTIVE STRUCTURES

There is inherent beauty in structural forms. Structures have a strong connection with geometry and math. Nature came out with three strategies to resist loads; Mass structures, shell structures and frame structures. In biological forms, nature adds material where is needed and removes material where is not required. With several millions of years in research and development, nature already solved the problems engineers are facing today. We can simulate this behaviour with the help of the finite element method and Bi-directional Evolutionary Topology Optimization (BESO). Beyond a computer simulation, digital fabrication will make possible to generate modular structural components that move across to resist loads as they change on the structural system.

Keywords: digital fabrication, finite element method. topology optimization, auxetic materials. deployable structures.

SUSTAINABLE BUILDINGS

Many people feel surprised when they learn that buildings and construction processes contribute to nearly 40% of the CO2 emissions and more than a third of the global energy consumption. I became LEED Accredited Proffesional in Buiding Design and Construction while I was leading a Hotel project located in Barcelona area. Spain. One day, I asked myself What if we create the most sustainable buiding ever designed?.

This is how Mim, a four star Hotel, run by a small family company, became the first LEED Platinum certified Hotel in Europe and the one with a higgest scorecard on Earth, without breaking the bank.

MICROSHELTERS

According to the Universal Declaration of Human Rights of the United Nations, everyone has the right to a standard of living adequate for the health and well-being of himself and of his family, including (...) housing. A typical building takes around two years to be built, involves huge human resources, time and money, resulting in an unaffordable privilege to a rising amount of people. Moreover inequality in the distribution of services across tne territory, paired with inflexible buiding codes generate even more demand and tension.

Project Nest is an innovative housing concept based on sustainability, technology and modular design. Initially aimed to the market of a single person, it grows and shrinks as life needs evolve. With the help of modern computing and digital fabrication, and a vast trajectory in traditional real estate, a group of talented people are set to change housing and communities to match human rights standards.
